Assignment 2 - How has the weather changed over the past 100 years?

Temperatures measured on land and at sea for more than a century show that Earth's globally averaged surface temperature is rising. Since 1970, global surface temperature rose at an average rate of about 0.17°C per decade—more than twice as fast as the 0.07°C per decade increase observed for the entire period of recorded observations (1880-2015). The average global temperature for 2016 was 0.94°C above the 20th century average of 13.9°C, surpassing the previous record warmth of 2015 by 0.04°C. Climate Change: Global Temperature, LuAnn Dahlman, September 11, 2017

Copenhagen (Annual temperatures)

St. Petersburg (Annual temperatures)

For these 2 visualizations, we computed annual min, max and average temperatures to show how annual temperature ranges differed from year to year in Copenhagen and St. Petersburg.

As expected, we can see signs of global warming by observing the trendline of average annual temperatures computed by least-squares fit. In Copenhagen, the temperature between years 1880-2011 changed by approx. 2 °C. In St. Petersburg, the temperature between years 1880-2016 changed by approx. 3 °C.

Zurich (Seasons)

This visualization allows us to observe the temperature changes, between 1880 and 2016, according to the different seasons of the year.

From these different line charts we notice a very slight increase in temperature for the winter, autumn and spring seasons. Nevertheless we realize that it is for the winter season that global warming is the most visible. Indeed, we notice that the temperature has on average increased by about 2 °C until today. These different line charts of the evolution of the temperature during the seasons also allow us to realize that on average some years were more rigorous than others and that there is a consequent temperature difference between the summer months and winter while the temperatures of the spring and autumn months remain almost identical.
