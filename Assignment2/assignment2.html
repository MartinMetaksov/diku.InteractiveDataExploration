Question 3.2 (Alex Lonchambon)

We want to illustrate the phenomenon of the increasing temperature due to global warming. Thus the addition of a dynamic map visualization could help to evaluate this temperature evolution. Take for example this data visualization, which illustrates 100 years of the Tour de France. We could realize a visualization similar to this one, which can allowed to visualize on a map the evolution of the temperature (associated with a color scheme going from blue for a cold temperature and judged in the norm, against a red color for a temperature considered hot and out of the norm) during a specific year.

Moreover, with a dynamic map visualization it would be possible to represent any other elements related to global warming, such as:

  • The evolution of the rising waters
  • The effect on animals and plants (migration, disappearance)
>

These elements could be added as streams or color boxes to the geographical area or could also be represented as statistical data in a side window.

This type of visualization could also make it possible to compare two years in particular.
