The map above allows us to get information about crime activities in San Francisco by interacting with the map's regions and three filters.

By interacting with each element one by one, we can see that:

  • 45% of total crime happens on Wednesday, Thursday and Friday with 15% crime per day which is slightly higher than 14% for other days.
  • From noon until midnight, the percentage of total crime incidents is equal to at least 20% for each time slot.
  • Larceny/theft represent the highest rate of total crime incidents (20%).
  • The neighborhood with most crime in total is the Southern neighborhood with 18% of all crime incidents. However, the Tenderloin neighborhood which is by far the smallest neighborhood, has 9% of total crime incidents which makes it the neighborhood with most crime per area.

By interacting with several elements at the same time, we can see that:

  • The Tenderloin neighborhood is mainly the neighborhood of drugs and narcotics and we can also see that most of these incidents take place after noon. But the fact that we can find drugs and narcotics here could also explain the other incidents such as assault, larceny/theft and other offenses.
  • Furthermore, the North-East neighborhoods (Tenderloin – Central – Northern - Southern) seem to be the ones that concentrate the highest proportion of incidents with a percentage of 49%. Thus, this region of San Francisco is surely the least safe.
